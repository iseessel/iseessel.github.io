About Me

I'm a 2017 graduate of Oberlin College. Teaching myself HTML and some Javascript before I was 10, I recently decided to pursue a career in tech. I excelled, getting an A+ in my Discrete Mathematics class, wherein we covered a variety of computer-science topics. After school, I decided to speed up my journey back into coding and enrolled in App Academy, a highly selective boot camp in New York City. After making a full-stack website from scratch, a chess API, and a Tetris browser-game, I am excited to continue learning new technologies and clever optimizations.

Coding Philosophy

I'm passionate about solving complex problems in efficient and elegant ways. I love to refactor messy code, find leaks that slow down performance, and continually improve upon past solutions. Whether discussing future code-to-be-written, existing code, or code written years ago, I always seek to grasp all the intricacies and details of the code, so that I can improve upon the base that has already been set.
